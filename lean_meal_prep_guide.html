Foundation
Fill Your Fridge
Having a well-stocked fridge is everything. When your kitchen is ready, healthy eating becomes effortless. Here are the key essentials we always recommend keeping on hand.
Everyday Fridge Essentials
Greek yoghurt – 0% / 2% lower cal options
Eggs – perfect for extra protein in preps & brekkie
Berries – blueberries, raspberries, strawberries
Salad greens – mixed salad, tomatoes, cucumber
Veggies – broccoli, green beans, carrots
Lean meats of your choice (options below)
Carbs of your choice (recommended options below)

💡 Pro Tip: Prep your fridge before your weekly cook. Knowing exactly what you have stops last-minute bad food decisions and saves you money.

02
The Method
The 6 Prep Steps
1
Ace Your Base
Every meal needs a quality carb to keep energy levels stable.
Wholemeal pasta
New potatoes / sweet potato
Brown rice / couscous
Wraps / protein pasta
2
Pick Your Protein
This is where you hit your daily protein goal. Lean options:
Chicken breast / turkey steaks
Turkey mince / turkey meatballs
Lean red mince (5% fat)
Tuna / white fish · Tofu
3
Choose Your Veg
Bulk out your meals for fibre and volume. Mix it up for variety.
Broccoli · Carrots · Green beans
Babycorn · Asparagus · Spinach
4
Add Flavour
Meal prep doesn't have to be boring. Low-cal seasonings (under 10 cals per serving):
Paprika · Chilli · Turmeric · Cumin
Nando's sauces · 10-cal mayo · Low-sugar ketchup
5
Keep It Simple
Don't try 5 different meals a week — it's overwhelming. Stick to 1 or 2 meals, rotate them through the week. Simple = sustainable.
6
Stock Up
Prep for 4 days max if storing in the fridge. We recommend Sunday → Thursday, then re-prep or cook fresh on weekends.

03
Rules
Before You Cook
Every recipe below follows the same simple approach. Keep these rules in mind and you'll always be on track.
Calories & protein are based on the exact grams stated. Adjust portions up or down to hit your personal daily targets.
Always pre-prep before you plan your week. Know your serving sizes and calories before you commit to a prep.
Track your food so you stay within your daily calorie target. Apps like MyFitnessPal make this simple.
Vegetarian swaps are noted for each recipe — just look for the (v) tag next to the protein ingredient.
These meals are fast, low-calorie, and high-protein. No complex techniques, no obscure ingredients — just results.
🔑
The Golden Rule Keep it simple, stay consistent. One solid prep beats five failed attempts at perfection every single time.

Lemon & Herb Fish with Minted Potatoes
278 kcal 20g protein
You Need
White fillet fish 135g
New potatoes 220g
Garlic powder, mixed herbs, black pepper
Lemon juice · Veggies of choice
Method
1
Chop potatoes, season, place in oven dish – cook 30 mins.
2
Wrap fish in foil with mixed herbs – oven 20 mins.
3
Cook veggies and serve!
Chicken Fried Rice Prep
490 kcal 20g protein
You Need
Chicken breast 130g (v: chicken-style pieces)
1 egg · Brown basmati rice 60g
Mixed greens – peas, pepper
Method
1
Cook rice in separate pan.
2
Chop & fry chicken in fry light, set aside.
3
Crack & scramble egg in pan, add chicken + rice.
4
Add veggies and serve.

Chicken & Couscous Salad
340 kcal 20g protein
You Need
Chicken breast 130g (v: chicken-style pieces)
Couscous 60g · 200ml low-salt veg stock
Cucumber, tomatoes, mixed salad, chicken spice
Method
1
Fry chicken with chicken spice – 15 mins.
2
Pour hot stock over couscous, cover 10 mins until fluffy.
3
Add chopped veg, cool chicken, combine and serve.

Chicken with Rice & Spinach
490 kcal 35g protein
You Need
Chicken breast 135g (v: chicken-style pieces)
Spinach handful · Chickpeas 50g
Basmati rice 60g · Carrot + spring onion + garlic
Method
1
Golden-brown chicken in fry light, set aside.
2
In same pan: spinach, chickpeas, carrot, rice + water. Cover + cook on medium until done.
3
Stir chicken back in, season and serve!
